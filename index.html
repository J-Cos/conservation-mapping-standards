How Accurate Is Your Conservation Map?

Maps without rigorous accuracy assessment risk misleading conservation decisions. This interactive demonstrator accompanies the conservation mapping standard proposed by Schulte to Bühne et al. (2026). Walk through the best-practice pipeline on synthetic data — then see what happens when common shortcuts are taken.

Spatial Blocking & Sampling Design

✓ Table 1: Independence & spatial validity
Pending

Divide the study area into spatial blocks to ensure training and validation data are independent. Nearby pixels are similar (spatially autocorrelated), so if they end up in both the training and test set, accuracy will be artificially inflated. By grouping pixels into blocks and splitting at the block level, we prevent this data leakage. Gold-highlighted blocks below are held out for validation in one example iteration (~36.8% of total).

3

Repeated Cross-Validation

✓ Table 1: Map accuracy & precision
Pending

A single train/test split can give a misleadingly precise accuracy estimate. Instead, draw B bootstrap samples of spatial blocks (with replacement). For each replicate, train a Random Forest on the drawn blocks and validate on the ~36.8% of blocks held out for testing. This produces B independent accuracy estimates from which distributions and confidence intervals are derived — revealing how certain we can be about map quality.
